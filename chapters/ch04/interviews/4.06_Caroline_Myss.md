**[Myss]**: That physical symptoms coordinate with emotional patterns and spiritual states in ways that rational medicine ignores. And that healing—real healing, not just symptom management—requires coordinating all three dimensions.

**[Interviewer]**: This sounds mystical. But you're saying it's empirical?

**[Myss]**: Thirty years of observation. Thousands of cases. Working directly with physicians who could verify the physical diagnoses while I read the emotional and spiritual patterns.

The pattern is consistent: Physical illness doesn't arise from physical causes alone. It emerges from coordination—or coordination failure—between physical, emotional, and spiritual dimensions.

**[Interviewer]**: Give us an example.

**[Myss]**: Two patients with identical cancer diagnoses. Same type, same stage, same treatment. One heals, one doesn't.

Medical science calls this "individual variation" and shrugs. But there's a pattern I've seen hundreds of times:

**The patient who heals** has coordinated all three dimensions:
- **Physical**: Follows medical treatment rigorously
- **Emotional**: Processes trauma, releases resentment, maintains hope
- **Spiritual**: Finds meaning in illness, feels connected to something larger, has purpose

**The patient who doesn't heal** has physical treatment but emotional/spiritual discord:
- Unprocessed trauma creating chronic stress hormones that suppress immune function
- Resentment and anger creating inflammation that accelerates disease
- Loss of meaning creating depression that undermines healing
- Isolation creating disconnection that prevents recovery

**[Interviewer]**: You're saying emotions and spirituality affect physical health?

**[Myss]**: Not mystically—coordinatively. This is psychoneuroimmunology. Emotional states coordinate with immune function. Spiritual connection coordinates with stress response. Physical health emerges from coordination of all three dimensions.

Western medicine optimizes physical treatment while ignoring emotional and spiritual dimensions. Alternative medicine addresses emotional/spiritual while ignoring physical reality. Both fail because healing requires coordinating all three.

## Act I: The Three Bodies of Healing (14 minutes)

**[Interviewer]**: Walk us through these three bodies—physical, emotional, spiritual. Start with the physical body.

**[Myss]**: The physical body is yang—active, measurable, treatable. Organs, systems, biochemistry. What doctors see and treat.

Western medicine understands this body brilliantly. Surgery, medications, procedures. They can replace hearts, remove tumors, regulate hormones. Technical mastery of the physical dimension.

But here's what they miss: The physical body doesn't exist in isolation. It coordinates with emotional and spiritual dimensions to create health or illness.

**Example**: Chronic pain. Doctors find nothing physically wrong. MRI is clean. Blood work normal. But the patient is suffering.

Western medicine says: "It's in your head" or "Learn to live with it."

What I see: Unprocessed emotional trauma stored in the body. The pain is real—it's the physical manifestation of emotional coordination failure.

**[Interviewer]**: Explain the emotional/mental body.

**[Myss]**: The emotional body is yin—receptive, experiential, often unconscious. Thoughts, feelings, beliefs, trauma. The invisible patterns that shape health.

This body holds your emotional history: childhood wounds, relationship patterns, belief systems, unprocessed trauma. And these patterns coordinate with physical health in measurable ways.

**Psychoneuroimmunology proves this**: 
- Chronic stress (emotional) suppresses immune function (physical)
- Depression (emotional) increases inflammation (physical)
- Trauma (emotional) alters gene expression (physical)
- Resentment (emotional) correlates with cancer progression (physical)

This isn't mysticism—it's science. Emotional states coordinate with physical health through measurable biological pathways.

**[Interviewer]**: How does this coordination work?

**[Myss]**: Through the stress response system, immune system, endocrine system. Your emotional state literally changes your biology.

**Example**: Chronic resentment. You hold anger toward someone who hurt you. That emotional pattern:
- Activates stress hormones continuously
- Suppresses immune surveillance (cancer cells aren't detected)
- Creates inflammation (disease accelerates)
- Disrupts sleep and digestion (healing capacity decreases)

The resentment isn't just emotional—it coordinates with physical biology to create disease.

**Or take hope**: Patients who maintain hope during cancer treatment have better outcomes. Not because hope cures cancer, but because hope coordinates with immune function, stress response, treatment compliance, social support. The emotional state coordinates with physical healing.

**[Interviewer]**: And the spiritual/energetic body?

**[Myss]**: The spiritual body is the Tao—the coordination space where physical and emotional create health or illness. Life force, meaning, purpose, connection.

This isn't religion—it's about meaning-making and connection. Do you feel your life has purpose? Are you connected to something larger than yourself? Does your suffering have meaning?

These spiritual dimensions coordinate with healing in profound ways.

**Viktor Frankl's research**: Holocaust survivors who found meaning in their suffering had dramatically better survival rates than those who didn't. Same physical conditions, same trauma. Different spiritual coordination.

**Modern research on meaning and health**: People with strong sense of purpose:
- Live longer
- Recover faster from illness
- Have stronger immune function
- Experience less pain
- Handle stress better

Why? Because meaning coordinates with biology. Purpose activates healing systems. Connection reduces stress response.

**[Interviewer]**: So all three bodies must coordinate for healing?

**[Myss]**: Exactly. You can't heal the body without addressing the emotions. You can't heal the emotions without finding meaning. You can't find meaning without treating the body.

Healing is coordination. When physical treatment coordinates with emotional processing coordinates with spiritual meaning-making, you create conditions for healing that medicine alone cannot achieve.

## Act II: Why Some People Heal and Others Don't (12 minutes)

**[Interviewer]**: You said two patients with identical diagnoses and treatment can have completely different outcomes. Walk us through the coordination patterns that determine who heals.

**[Myss]**: I've watched this pattern hundreds of times. The difference isn't medical—it's coordinative.

**Case study: Two breast cancer patients**

**Patient A—Coordination success**:

**Physical layer**: Undergoes surgery, chemotherapy, radiation. Follows all medical protocols. Takes care of body through nutrition, rest, gentle exercise.

**Emotional layer**: Enters therapy to process childhood trauma that she recognizes contributed to disease. Releases resentment toward mother. Forgives ex-husband. Allows herself to feel and express emotions she'd suppressed for decades. Builds supportive relationships.

**Spiritual layer**: Finds meaning in illness—sees it as wake-up call to live authentically. Reconnects with spiritual practice. Discovers purpose in helping other cancer patients. Feels part of something larger than herself.

**Coordination**: Physical treatment coordinates with emotional healing coordinates with spiritual transformation. Each dimension supports the others.

**Outcome**: Complete remission. Thriving ten years later. Not just cancer-free—transformed. Healthier emotionally and spiritually than before diagnosis.

**Patient B—Coordination failure**:

**Physical layer**: Same treatment as Patient A. Same medical care. Same physical intervention.

**Emotional layer**: Refuses to address emotional patterns. "I just want to fight the cancer." Suppresses fear and anger. Maintains resentment toward family members. Isolates emotionally. Denies that emotions affect health.

**Spiritual layer**: Sees illness as random bad luck. No meaning-making. Loses sense of purpose. Feels disconnected and alone. Existential despair.

**Coordination failure**: Physical treatment without emotional processing or spiritual meaning. Each dimension works against the others.

**Outcome**: Cancer returns within two years. Physical treatment becomes less effective. Patient deteriorates despite medical intervention.

**[Interviewer]**: You're saying Patient B's emotional and spiritual state caused the cancer to return?

**[Myss]**: Not caused—coordinated. The cancer returned because healing conditions weren't created.

Think of it this way: Physical treatment kills cancer cells. But it doesn't change the internal environment that allowed cancer to develop. If that environment—created by emotional patterns and spiritual disconnection—remains unchanged, cancer often returns.

**Patient A** changed the internal environment. Emotional healing reduced chronic stress. Spiritual transformation created purpose and connection. These changes coordinated with physical treatment to create conditions where healing could occur.

**Patient B** only treated symptoms. The underlying coordination failure—emotional suppression and spiritual disconnection—continued. Physical treatment alone couldn't overcome coordination failure.

**[Interviewer]**: This is psychoneuroimmunology?

**[Myss]**: Yes. The science is clear:

**Chronic stress** (emotional) → Cortisol elevation (biochemical) → Immune suppression (physical) → Cancer progression (disease)

**Unprocessed trauma** (emotional) → Inflammation (biochemical) → Tissue damage (physical) → Chronic illness (disease)

**Loss of meaning** (spiritual) → Depression (emotional) → Immune dysfunction (biochemical) → Disease susceptibility (physical)

These aren't separate systems—they're coordinated systems. Emotional and spiritual states literally change your biology through measurable pathways.

**[Interviewer]**: But Western medicine doesn't address emotional and spiritual dimensions.

**[Myss]**: That's why Western medicine often fails at healing even when it succeeds at treatment.

They can remove tumors, kill cancer cells, manage symptoms. Technical success. But if they don't address the emotional patterns and spiritual disconnection that contributed to disease, healing is incomplete.

**The patient is "cured" but not healed**. The cancer is gone but the person is still sick—emotionally suppressed, spiritually disconnected, living the same patterns that created disease.

And often, disease returns. Because coordination failure continues.

**[Myss]**: Cancer treatment that coordinates all three dimensions.

**Physical layer—Allopathic medicine**:

- Chemotherapy to kill cancer cells
- Radiation to destroy remaining cells
- Medication to manage symptoms

**Emotional layer—Psychotherapy**:
- Process trauma that suppressed immune function
- Release resentment that created inflammation
- Address fear and anger that disrupt healing
- Build emotional support systems
- Learn healthy emotional expression

**Spiritual layer—Meaning-making practices**:
- Find purpose in illness (wake-up call, transformation opportunity)
- Reconnect with spiritual practice (meditation, prayer, nature)
- Discover meaning through service (helping others)
- Cultivate gratitude and connection
- Transform relationship with mortality

**The coordination**: Physical treatment creates space for healing. Emotional work removes blocks to healing. Spiritual transformation activates healing capacity. All three coordinate to create conditions for recovery.

**[Interviewer]**: Do patients who receive coordinated treatment have better outcomes?

**[Myss]**: Yes. And research is starting to prove it.

**Studies on psychosocial interventions in cancer**:
- Support groups improve survival rates
- Stress reduction enhances treatment effectiveness
- Meaning-making practices reduce pain and improve quality of life
- Emotional expression correlates with better immune function

Not because emotions cure cancer—but because emotional and spiritual healing coordinate with physical treatment to create optimal conditions for recovery.

**[Interviewer]**: What becomes possible when medicine recognizes that humans aren't machines with broken parts—we're coordination systems where physical, emotional, and spiritual dimensions create health or illness together?

**[Myss]**: Healing that serves the whole person, not just treats symptoms.

**Preventive medicine** that addresses emotional patterns before they manifest as disease.

**Treatment protocols** that coordinate physical intervention with emotional healing with spiritual transformation.

**Healthcare practitioners** who understand that their role is facilitating coordination, not just treating bodies.

**Patients** who are empowered to participate in their healing through emotional work and spiritual practice, not just passive recipients of medical intervention.

This is the future of medicine: recognizing that healing is coordination. Physical treatment coordinates with emotional healing coordinates with spiritual transformation to create recovery that serves human flourishing.

## Closing: Holistic Medicine's True Meaning (4 minutes)

**[Interviewer]**: "Holistic medicine" is often seen as alternative to scientific medicine. But you're saying it's coordination of all dimensions that create human health. What's the difference?

**[Myss]**: Holistic doesn't mean alternative. It means integrated. Coordinated.

**Alternative medicine** often rejects physical treatment in favor of emotional/spiritual approaches. That's just optimization of different dimensions—still fails.

**Allopathic medicine** rejects emotional/spiritual dimensions in favor of physical treatment. Optimization of physical layer—also fails.

**True holistic medicine** coordinates all three: physical treatment with emotional healing with spiritual transformation.

Not alternative to scientific medicine—coordination of scientific medicine with emotional wisdom with spiritual practice.

**[Interviewer]**: What's the most important thing medical professionals need to understand about healing as coordination?

**[Myss]**: That healing and curing are different.

**Curing** is eliminating disease. You can cure someone and not heal them. Remove the tumor but leave the person emotionally devastated and spiritually empty.

**Healing** is restoring wholeness. Coordinating physical health with emotional well-being with spiritual connection.

The best medicine does both: treats disease while facilitating healing. Coordinates physical intervention with emotional support with spiritual meaning-making.

And here's what thirty years has taught me: When you coordinate all three dimensions, healing happens at levels that medicine alone cannot achieve.

Physical recovery is faster. Pain is reduced. Quality of life is better. And even when cure isn't possible, healing still occurs—people find peace, meaning, connection in the face of mortality.

This is the wisdom that medicine needs: Humans aren't machines to be fixed. We're coordination systems where physical, emotional, and spiritual dimensions create health or illness together.

Healing emerges from coordination. And the physician's role—the healer's role—is facilitating that coordination.
