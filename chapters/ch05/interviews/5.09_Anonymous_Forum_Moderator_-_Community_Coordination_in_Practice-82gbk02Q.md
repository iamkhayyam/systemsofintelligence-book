## Pre-Interview Context

### Who is Mod Alex?

Mod Alex is the invisible coordinator. Behind every thriving online community is someone (usually multiple people) doing the exhausting work of maintaining coordination health. Most users never see this labor. But without it, communities descend into chaos or toxicity within weeks.

**Background:**

- 10+ years moderating online communities


- Started as community member, became mod, now senior mod


- Moderates across platforms: Reddit (multiple subreddits), Discord servers, independent forums


- Communities range from 10,000 to 500,000 members


- Unpaid volunteer work: 15-20 hours weekly


- Has seen communities thrive and die based on coordination health



**Why their voice matters:**
They're living Wales's Wikipedia principles at smaller scale. They're applying Pentland's coordination optimization daily. They're dealing with Harris's platform design failures. They're practicing Vallor's virtue ethics in real-time. And they're burning out from the coordination labor that makes digital community possible.

This is coordination work made visible. The invisible labor that enables collective intelligence.

### The Stakes

Online communities are where humanity coordinates at scale. Millions of communities across platforms, billions of people participating. These communities are coordination systems—and their health depends on moderation.

But moderation is:

- Unpaid labor (volunteers doing 10-40 hours weekly)


- Invisible work (users don't see it when it's working)


- Emotionally exhausting (constant exposure to worst human behavior)


- Technically unsupported (platforms provide terrible tools)


- Culturally unappreciated (users complain, rarely thank)



This matters because:

1. **Community health depends on moderation** - Without it, communities become toxic or die


2. **Moderators are burning out** - Turnover is 1-2 years on average


3. **Platforms exploit mod labor** - Billion-dollar companies built on unpaid coordination work


4. **AI could help** - But only if designed to facilitate coordination, not replace judgment



This interview reveals the reality of community coordination—and what's needed to make it sustainable.

### Interview Strategy

This interview follows a three-act structure from daily reality to coordination challenges to future needs:

**Act I (10 min):** The coordination reality—what moderation actually is
**Act II (18 min):** Coordination in practice—onboarding, conflict, culture maintenance
**Act III (10 min):** The future—AI assistance, sustainability, vision

The key is authenticity. No sanitizing, no heroic narrative. Just honest sharing of what coordination work actually requires—the exhaustion, the frustration, the small victories, the burnout risk. This is the human cost of maintaining coordination health.

### Coordinating Conflict (6 minutes)

**INTERVIEWER:**
"Conflict is inevitable when people disagree. How do you coordinate conflict productively rather than destructively?"

**[VISUAL: Examples of conflict threads—both destructive and productive]**

*[Expected response: Conflict isn't the problem—uncoordinated conflict is the problem. My job is coordinating conflict toward productive resolution rather than destructive escalation. Let me show you the difference.]*

**Destructive Conflict Coordination:**

**The scenario:**

- Two users disagree about topic


- User A: "X is obviously true"


- User B: "No, Y is true, you're wrong"



**Without intervention:**

**Escalation Phase 1:**

- Personal attacks begin


- "You're an idiot"


- "You clearly don't understand this"


- Focus shifts from issue to identity



**Escalation Phase 2:**

- Others pile on, taking sides


- Thread becomes flame war


- Multiple people attacking each other


- Coordination toward hostility



**Escalation Phase 3:**

- Both original users leave angry


- Observers learn: disagreement = hostility


- Community culture becomes more hostile


- **Coordination failure creating toxicity**



**[VISUAL: Thread showing escalation from disagreement to flame war]**

**The coordination analysis:**

- Individual conflict coordinates with


- Platform visibility (everyone sees the fight) coordinates with


- Social dynamics (others join in) to create


- Collective toxicity


- **Destructive coordination cascade**



**Productive Conflict Coordination:**

**Same scenario, but I intervene:**

**Intervention 1: Separate Behavior from Person**

**My post:**

- "You both have valid perspectives. Let's focus on the issue, not each other."


- Redirect from identity to ideas


- **Coordinate attention toward substance**



**Intervention 2: Establish Ground Rules**

**My post:**

- "Let's disagree respectfully. No personal attacks. Focus on evidence and reasoning."


- Set coordination norms for this discussion


- **Coordinate behavior toward productivity**



**Intervention 3: Facilitate Understanding**

**My post:**

- "Alex, can you explain your reasoning? Jordan, what specifically concerns you about that view?"


- Draw out actual positions


- **Coordinate toward mutual understanding**



**Intervention 4: Find Common Ground**

**My post:**

- "You both agree on [X]. The disagreement is about [Y]. What evidence would change your view?"


- Identify shared ground


- Focus on actual disagreement


- **Coordinate toward resolution or respectful disagreement**



**Intervention 5: Model Desired Behavior**

**My participation:**

- I engage in discussion


- Demonstrate respectful disagreement


- Show how to change mind based on evidence


- **Coordinate by example**



**[VISUAL: Same thread with moderation interventions, showing productive resolution]**

**The coordination outcome:**

- Both users feel heard


- Observers learn how to disagree respectfully


- Community norms reinforced


- Culture becomes more robust


- **Coordination creating value from conflict**



**MOD ALEX:**
*[The difference isn't eliminating conflict—it's coordinating conflict productively. Disagreement can strengthen community if coordinated well. It destroys community if coordinated poorly. My job is facilitating productive coordination.]*

### AI and the Future of Moderation (3 minutes)

**INTERVIEWER:**
"Could AI help with moderation? What would actually be useful vs. what would make things worse?"

**[VISUAL: Comparison of helpful vs. unhelpful AI moderation approaches]**

**MOD ALEX:**
*[Expected response: AI could help with coordination—but only if designed correctly. Most AI moderation makes things worse because it tries to replace human judgment instead of facilitating human coordination.]*

**What I DON'T Need: AI Decision-Making**

**The approach:**

- AI automatically removes content or bans users


- Based on algorithms, no human judgment


- Supposed to reduce mod workload



**Why it fails:**

- **Context matters**: Same word can be appropriate or offensive depending on context


- **Intent matters**: Is this harassment or banter between friends?


- **Culture matters**: Different communities have different norms


- AI can't understand these nuances


- **Coordination requires judgment**



**The outcomes:**

- False positives: Removing good content


- False negatives: Missing real problems


- User frustration: Unfair automated decisions


- Mod frustration: Cleaning up AI mistakes


- **Coordination failure**



**What I DO Need: AI Coordination Facilitation**

**Priority Sorting:**

- AI analyzes reports


- Flags most urgent: "This looks like coordinated harassment"


- Deprioritizes likely false reports


- I handle real problems first


- **Coordinate attention effectively**



**Pattern Recognition:**

- AI detects emerging issues


- "Sudden spike in hostile comments about Topic X"


- "This user's behavior matches previous ban evaders"


- Early warning enables early intervention


- **Coordinate proactive response**



**Context Provision:**

- AI provides relevant context when I'm reviewing


- "This user has 3 previous warnings"


- "This phrase often used in harassment campaigns"


- Informed decision-making


- **Coordinate judgment with information**



**Sentiment Tracking:**

- AI monitors community sentiment


- "Frustration increasing about Policy Y"


- "This thread becoming heated"


- Enables proactive intervention


- **Coordinate preventive action**



**[VISUAL: AI assistance interface showing each facilitation type]**

**MOD ALEX:**
*[I need AI that enhances my coordination capabilities, not that replaces my judgment. Because moderation is fundamentally about coordinating human behavior with community culture. That requires human understanding of context, intent, and nuance. AI can help me coordinate more effectively. It can't coordinate for me.]*

## ACT III: THE FUTURE OF COMMUNITY COORDINATION (10 minutes)

**Principle 1: Clear, Consistent Culture**

- Members understand what behavior is expected

- Consistently reinforced


- Openly discussed and evolved



**Thriving communities:**

- Clear culture, well-documented


- Consistently enforced by mods and members


- Regular discussion of norms


- Culture adapts to community needs


- **Coordination clarity**

- Unclear or inconsistent culture


- Arbitrary enforcement


- Hidden norms (insiders know, newcomers don't)


- Culture rigid or chaotic


- **Coordination confusion**



**The implementation:**

- Document norms clearly


- Enforce consistently


- Explain reasoning when enforcing


- Welcome discussion of norms


- **Transparent coordination**



**Principle 2: Distributed Coordination**

**What it means:**

- Moderation can't just be mods


- Community must coordinate itself


- Shared responsibility for health


- Members as coordination partners



**Thriving communities:**

- Members welcome newcomers


- Users report problems


- Experienced members mentor new ones


- Community discusses and shapes norms


- **Distributed coordination ownership**



**Dying communities:**

- Mods vs. users (adversarial)


- No shared ownership


- Users passive, mods exhausted


- **Centralized coordination failure**

- Empower users to help moderate


- Recognize positive contributions


- Include community in governance


- Build culture of shared responsibility


- **Coordination as collective practice**



**Principle 3: Adaptive Governance**

**What it means:**

- Communities evolve


- What worked at 100 members doesn't work at 10,000


- Norms must adapt


- Governance must evolve



**Thriving communities:**

- Regular reflection on what's working


- Norm evolution based on experience


- Community input on governance


- Adaptive coordination


- **Learning coordination system**



**Dying communities:**

- Rigid rules, no adaptation


- Governance doesn't scale


- Mods burn out from unchanging approach


- **Static coordination failure**



**[VISUAL: Community health metrics showing correlation with these three principles]**

**MOD ALEX:**
