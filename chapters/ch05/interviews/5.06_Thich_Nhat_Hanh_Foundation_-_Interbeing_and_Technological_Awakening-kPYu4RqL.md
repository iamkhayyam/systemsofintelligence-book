## Pre-Interview Context

### Who is Sister True Virtue?

Sister True Virtue represents a lineage of wisdom 2,500 years old, applied to challenges 25 years new. Trained by Thich Nhat Hanh—the Vietnamese Zen master who brought mindfulness to the West—she carries forward his teaching of "interbeing": that nothing exists independently, everything inter-is with everything else.

**Key background:**

- Ordained as Buddhist nun at Plum Village (2009)


- Trained directly by Thich Nhat Hanh before his passing (2022)


- Senior dharma teacher leading retreats worldwide


- Specializes in applying mindfulness to technology challenges


- Leads Plum Village's "Mindful Technology" programs


- Works with tech companies on contemplative approaches to design


- Author of articles on Buddhist perspectives on AI and technology



**Why her voice matters:**
She's not a technologist critiquing from Buddhist principles—she's a contemplative practitioner who uses technology and sees clearly how it coordinates with consciousness. Her insights come from direct experience: watching how phones interrupt presence, how social media creates suffering, how technology can support or undermine awakening. This is wisdom tested in practice.

### The Stakes

The Buddha taught that suffering comes from three poisons: greed (craving), aversion (rejection), and delusion (not seeing clearly). Technology amplifies all three. Social media coordinates with craving (more likes, more content, more validation). Algorithms coordinate with aversion (outrage, filter bubbles, tribal warfare). Constant distraction coordinates with delusion (preventing clear seeing).

But Buddhist wisdom also teaches: Suffering can be transformed. Awakening is possible. And technology can support awakening if we coordinate with it wisely.

This matters because:

1. **Individual**: Mindful technology use can reduce suffering and support practice


2. **Collective**: Communities practicing together can create alternative coordination patterns


3. **Systemic**: Technology designed for awakening instead of exploitation is possible


4. **Universal**: The Buddha's teaching of interbeing is the three-body problem in contemplative form



This interview brings 2,500 years of wisdom to bear on the coordination challenges of the AI age.

### Interview Strategy

This interview follows a three-act structure mirroring the dharma teaching method:

**Act I (10 min):** The teaching of interbeing—nothing exists independently, everything coordinates
**Act II (20 min):** The three poisons in technology—how platforms amplify suffering
**Act III (12 min):** The path to technological awakening—practices for wise coordination

The key is spaciousness. Don't rush the wisdom. Include silence, include bells, include breathing space. Let insights land before moving forward. This is contemplative television—a different pace, a different quality of attention.
