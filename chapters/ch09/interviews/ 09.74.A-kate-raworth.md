## Pre-Interview Context

**Who is Kate Raworth:**
- Senior Research Associate at Oxford University's Environmental Change Institute
- Creator of Doughnut Economics model
- Author of "Doughnut Economics: Seven Ways to Think Like a 21st-Century Economist"
- Advisor to governments and cities on regenerative economic transformation
- Champion of economics that coordinates human wellbeing with planetary boundaries

**Why This Interview Matters:**
This interview scales coordination theory to planetary level, showing how our economic crisis is fundamentally a coordination failure: GDP growth optimizes one metric without coordinating with ecological boundaries or human wellbeing. Raworth's Doughnut provides a visual framework for three-body economic coordination—meeting human needs within planetary limits.

**Key Themes:**
- Doughnut as coordination framework
- Three bodies: ecology, humanity, economy
- Regenerative and distributive by design
- Amsterdam circular economy example
- AI as coordination opportunity or amplification threat
