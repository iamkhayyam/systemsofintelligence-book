## Pre-Interview Context

**Who is Martina Menegon:**
- Community organizer in Brooklyn mutual aid networks
- 8+ years organizing grassroots community support systems, starting with tenant rights and anti-displacement work.
- COVID-19 mutual aid network coordinator (2020-2022), specifically in Sunset Park and Bay Ridge, Brooklyn.
- Platform cooperative advocate, deeply involved in the movement for democratic ownership of digital tools.
- Bridge between community organizing and technology coordination, passionate about designing systems that serve people, not profit.

**Why This Interview Matters:**
This interview demonstrates coordination at community scale, showing how mutual aid coordinates individual needs with collective capacity through simple, human-centered infrastructure. Menegon's COVID work proves that communities have enormous capacity when coordination infrastructure exists, and reveals the critical difference between technology that serves coordination vs. technology that extracts from community. It's a powerful argument for a more equitable, resilient future built on solidarity, not exploitation.

**Key Themes:**
- Three bodies: individual needs, community capacity, coordination infrastructure
- Mutual aid vs. charity vs. market: fundamental differences in power dynamics and community building.
- COVID mutual aid explosion: a real-world stress test revealing inherent community resilience.
- Platform cooperatives vs. platform capitalism: contrasting models of technological ownership and value distribution.
- Technology serving community, not extracting from it: a call for ethical design and democratic control.

**Serve coordination:** It enables the community to help itself. The technology disappears into the background, and the community is strengthened.

- Replace human connection: A platform as an intermediary turns a relationship into a transaction, weakening community.

### Segment 9: The Platform Cooperative Model (29:00 - 33:00)

**Members own the platform:** This means democratic governance—one member, one vote. Collective decision-making. The users are the bosses.
**Value stays in the community:** There are no external shareholders to extract profits. Any surplus is either reinvested in the platform or distributed back to the members. Wealth builds locally.
