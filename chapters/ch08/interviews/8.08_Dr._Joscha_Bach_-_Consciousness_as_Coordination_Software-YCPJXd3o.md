### Segment 5: The Three Bodies of Conscious Computation [12:00-20:00] (8 min)

**Key Question:**
"Walk us through your model. How do sensory processing, world modeling, and attention coordinate to create consciousness?"

**Content Points:**

**Body 1: Sensory Processing (Input Layer)**

**What it does:**
- Perception of environment
- Pattern recognition in sensory streams
- Information intake from world

**How it works:**
- Visual: Edge detection, object recognition, scene understanding
- Auditory: Sound processing, speech recognition, music perception
- **Other senses:** Touch, proprioception, etc.

**What flows in:**
- Raw sensory data
- Millions of bits per second
- **Massive:** Parallel processing

**Consciousness status:**
- **Mostly unconscious:** You don't experience the processing
- Only results reach consciousness

**Example:**
- Reading this text
- Eyes scanning letters (unconscious)
- **Conscious:** Only the meaning, not the mechanics

**Body 2: World Modeling (Representation Layer)**

**What it does:**
- Internal simulation of reality
- Predictive models of how world works
- Conceptual structures and knowledge

**How it works:**
- Build models of objects, people, causality
- Predict what happens next
- **Simulation:** Running models to imagine scenarios

**What's represented:**
- Meaning, relationships, causality
- Past, present, future
- **Abstract:** Concepts and ideas

**Consciousness status:**
- **Some conscious:** Active models
- **Most unconscious:** Stored knowledge
- Only currently active models are conscious

**Example:**
- Understanding a sentence
- Activating language models (unconscious)
- **Conscious:** The meaning that emerges

**Body 3: Attention/Awareness (Coordination Layer)**

**What it does:**
- Determines which models are active
- What's being simulated in working memory
- **The spotlight:** Of conscious processing

**How it works:**
- Selects relevant information from sensory input
- Activates relevant world models
- **Coordinates:** Perception with understanding

**What's conscious:**
- Only what attention coordinates with world models
- **The coordination:** Creates conscious experience
- Everything else: Unconscious

**Example:**
- Reading this text
- **Sensory:** Eyes scanning (unconscious)
- **Models:** Language understanding (unconscious)
- **Attention:** Coordinates both (conscious)
- **You experience:** The coordinated meaning, not the components

**The coordination creates consciousness:**
- You're not conscious of everything your brain processes
- **Only:** What attention coordinates with world models
- **Consciousness IS:** The coordination
- Not the components—the coordination between them

**Visual moment:**
- Diagram showing three layers
- Illustrate: Attention coordinating sensory input with world models
- Emphasize: Consciousness is coordination, not components
